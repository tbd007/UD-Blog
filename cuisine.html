    Croatian Art of Cooking

    “Sample delicate seafood dishes, hearty ham and rich pastries on a picturesque coast”
    -Kristin Vukovic

    Black Risotto

    Locally known as "crni rižot" , this dish is made with cuttlefish or squid, olive oil, garlic, red wine and squid ink, which gives it a great seafood flavour and black colour.

    Ox Carpaccio

    The white-grey, long-horned Istrian oxen are a gourmet delicacy. Boškarin is served at top restaurants and konobas (taverns) in a variety of ways, including as carpaccio;

    Buzara

    This simple dish of mussels cooked in a wine broth with garlic and breadcrumbs is very popular on the Croatian coast. The word Buzara means 'stew'.

    Brodetto

    This fisherman stew is made over open fire using the catch of the day, with a tomato base and applying apple vinegar to preserve the meal for a couple of days.

    Fritule

    These donut-like fried pastries can be made with egg yolks, raisins, grated lemon or orange rinds, and even rum.
    Be cautious - Highly addictive!

    Raw Seafood - Oyster

    Often called 'Mediterranean sashimi', oysters drizzled with olive oil create a tasteful moment with oysters from Ston, prized for their intense, briny flavour.
